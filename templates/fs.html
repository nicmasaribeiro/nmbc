Calculate f(s)

The formula for \( f(s) \) is:

\[ f(s) = s^2 B(s)^2 - 2s B(s) \int_{0}^{s} B(t) \, dt + \int_{0}^{s} \left( \int_{0}^{s} B(x)^2 \, dx \right) \, dy \]

Explanation

The function \( f(s) \) models a system where random noise or fluctuations affect the behavior of the system over time. It captures the relationship between the variable \( s \), which may represent time or space, and the probability distribution \( B(s) \), which models random effects using a normal (Gaussian) distribution with a variance inversely proportional to \( s \). The formula integrates both the local and cumulative effects of this stochastic noise.

The first term \( s^2 B(s)^2 \) represents the squared magnitude of noise at the point \( s \), while the second term captures the interaction between noise at \( s \) and its integrated history up to \( s \). The third term accounts for the cumulative squared noise over the interval from 0 to \( s \). Overall, this equation models how stochastic effects accumulate and interact, influencing a system's behavior.

This function is particularly useful in fields like stochastic processes, signal processing, and financial modeling, where random fluctuations (noise) play a critical role.
