Derivation of the Black-Scholes Equation

Assumptions

  1. The price of the underlying asset \( S \) follows a geometric Brownian motion: \[ dS = \mu S \, dt + \sigma S \, dW, \] where:
    • \( \mu \) is the drift rate,
    • \( \sigma \) is the volatility,
    • \( W \) is a Wiener process.
  2. No arbitrage opportunities exist in the market.
  3. Trading is frictionless (no transaction costs, no taxes, continuous trading).
  4. The risk-free interest rate \( r \) is constant.
  5. The option is European, exercisable only at maturity.

Step 1: Portfolio Replication

We construct a portfolio \( \Pi \) consisting of:

  • \( \Delta \) units of the underlying asset \( S \),
  • A short position in one option (value \( V(S, t) \)).

The portfolio value is: \[ \Pi = \Delta S - V(S, t). \]

The change in the portfolio value over a small time interval \( dt \) is: \[ d\Pi = \Delta \, dS - dV. \]

Step 2: Apply Ito's Lemma to \( V(S, t) \)

Using Ito's lemma for \( V(S, t) \), where \( V \) is a function of both \( S \) and \( t \): \[ dV = \frac{\partial V}{\partial t} \, dt + \frac{\partial V}{\partial S} \, dS + \frac{1}{2} \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S^2} \sigma^2 S^2 \, dt. \]

Substitute \( dS = \mu S \, dt + \sigma S \, dW \) into \( dV \): \[ dV = \frac{\partial V}{\partial t} \, dt + \frac{\partial V}{\partial S} (\mu S \, dt + \sigma S \, dW) + \frac{1}{2} \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S^2} \sigma^2 S^2 \, dt. \]

Simplify: \[ dV = \left( \frac{\partial V}{\partial t} + \mu S \frac{\partial V}{\partial S} + \frac{1}{2} \sigma^2 S^2 \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S^2} \right) dt + \sigma S \frac{\partial V}{\partial S} \, dW. \]

Step 3: Eliminate Risk (Hedging)

To eliminate risk (the stochastic term \( dW \)), choose \( \Delta = \frac{\partial V}{\partial S} \). Substituting \( \Delta dS \) and \( dV \) into \( d\Pi \): \[ d\Pi = \Delta \, dS - dV = \frac{\partial V}{\partial S} (\mu S \, dt + \sigma S \, dW) - \left( \frac{\partial V}{\partial t} \, dt + \frac{\partial V}{\partial S} (\mu S \, dt + \sigma S \, dW) + \frac{1}{2} \sigma^2 S^2 \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S^2} \, dt \right). \]

After simplifying: \[ d\Pi = -\left( \frac{\partial V}{\partial t} + \frac{1}{2} \sigma^2 S^2 \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S^2} \right) dt. \]

Step 4: Arbitrage-Free Condition

In an arbitrage-free market, the portfolio \( \Pi \) must earn the risk-free rate \( r \): \[ d\Pi = r \Pi \, dt. \]

Substitute \( \Pi = \Delta S - V = \frac{\partial V}{\partial S} S - V \): \[ -\left( \frac{\partial V}{\partial t} + \frac{1}{2} \sigma^2 S^2 \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S^2} \right) dt = r \left( \frac{\partial V}{\partial S} S - V \right) dt. \]

Simplify: \[ \frac{\partial V}{\partial t} + \frac{1}{2} \sigma^2 S^2 \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S^2} + r S \frac{\partial V}{\partial S} - r V = 0. \]

Black-Scholes Equation

The Black-Scholes PDE is: \[ \frac{\partial V}{\partial t} + \frac{1}{2} \sigma^2 S^2 \frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S^2} + r S \frac{\partial V}{\partial S} - r V = 0. \]
