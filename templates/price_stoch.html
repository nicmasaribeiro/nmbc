The formula for \( P₀(t, r, u, \rho, n) \) is:

\[ P₀(t, r, u, \rho, n) = F(t) \left(S(t, r) S_0 - K e^{-rt}\right) + \exp(r) F(t) \left(1 - \frac{F(t)}{K}\right) + \exp\left(\left(u - \frac{\rho^2}{2}\right)t + \rho B(t)\right) d_t \]

Explanation of Variables

In the context of this equation, the following variables are used:

  • t: The time variable, typically measured in years. It represents the time horizon over which the calculation is made.
  • r: The risk-free interest rate. This is the rate of return on an investment with no risk, typically a government bond rate.
  • u: The drift rate, representing the expected rate of return of the underlying asset.
  • ρ (rho): The volatility parameter that represents the correlation between the underlying asset's returns and stochastic noise. Higher \( ρ \) means more randomness.
  • S₀: The initial price of the underlying asset (e.g., a stock or commodity).
  • K: The strike price of the option. This is the price at which the option can be exercised.
  • dt: A small time increment for approximating the behavior of the stochastic process.
  • B(t): A Brownian motion (or stochastic process) term that models random fluctuations over time.
  • F(t): Represents the square of the function \( f(t) \), which is related to the accumulated noise in the system.
  • S(t, r): A function representing the interaction between time \( t \), interest rate \( r \), and the stochastic process affecting the system.
