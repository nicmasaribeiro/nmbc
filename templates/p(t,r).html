Calculate P(t, r), Reversion, and Spread Coefficient

The formula for \( P(t, r) \) is:

\[ P(t, r) = -F(t) \left(S(t, r) S_0 - K e^{-rt}\right) + F(t)\left(1 - \frac{F(t)}{K}\right)N(t, r) \]

Explanation of Variables

  • t: Time variable, typically measured in years.
  • r: The interest rate or reversion rate in the system.
  • S₀: The initial stock price, representing the current price of the asset.
  • K: Strike price, representing the price at which the option can be exercised.
  • dt: A small time increment for approximating the behavior of the stochastic process.
  • F(t): Represents a complex stochastic function based on \( f(t) \), often related to volatility or accumulated noise in the system.
  • S(t, r): A function that captures the interaction between time, interest rate, and stochastic processes.
  • N(t, r): A random process modeling noise or randomness in the system.
