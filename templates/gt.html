Calculate and Simulate g(t, r)

The formula for \( g(t, r) \) is:

\[ g(t, r) = (1 - r^2) e^{r f(t)} \]

Explanation

The function \( g(t, r) \) represents a stochastic growth coefficient that models how random fluctuations influence growth over time. It has two primary variables:

  • \( t \) – typically represents time or some progressive process. As time changes, the value of \( g(t, r) \) evolves, reflecting growth or decay with stochastic effects.
  • \( r \) – represents the randomness or noise factor. When \( r = 0 \), the function behaves deterministically (i.e., with no random influence). As \( r \) increases, stochasticity becomes more prominent in the model, simulating the effect of randomness on the system's growth.

In summary, \( g(t, r) \) describes a system where random fluctuations impact its growth. The balance between deterministic and stochastic behavior is controlled by \( r \), while \( t \) shows how the system evolves over time.
